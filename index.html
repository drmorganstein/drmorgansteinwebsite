About

Dr. Penni Morganstein provides individual and group counseling, workshops, and administrative consultation on a University level at The New York School of Interior Design and Sotheby’s Institute of Art Masters Programs.

In addition, she meets with patients privately in downtown Brooklyn and remotely via teletherapy. Dr. Morganstein earned her PsyD from Pace University, a Master’s in Education from Hunter College and was a Fellow at the Institute for Educational Leadership at New York University.

Services

Dr. Morganstein provides individual therapy in a warm, nurturing environment to children, adolescents and adults. Her integrative therapeutic approach, based on years of therapeutic experience, combines psychodynamic, insight-oriented, mindfulness, and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) theories, designed to suit each patient’s diagnosis and individual needs.

Her areas of expertise and specialization include, but are not limited to, depression, generalized and social anxiety and panic attacks. Dr. Morganstein also has experience with parenting and relationship issues, as well as life transitions. She has helped patients adjust to relocation to and from NYC, separation & divorce, career changes, returning to work or school after a hiatus, and develop strategies to improve time and stress management. She provides an open minded, culturally sensitive environment and attracts people of diverse backgrounds, who hail from across the United States and all over the world.

Additionally, Dr. Morganstein works with individuals in the arts and design worlds, helping them move their careers forward or unblock artistically and tap into their creativity.

Dr. Penni Morganstein provides individual and group therapy, trainings and workshops for primary, secondary and University students, educators and administrators.
